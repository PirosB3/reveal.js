What is Sentiment Analysis

  • It's software for automatically extracting opinions, emotions and sentiment in text
  • It allows us to track attitudes and feelings on the web. People write blog posts, comments, reviews and tweets about all sorts of different topics.
  • It allows us to track products, brands, and people to determine whether they are viewed positively or negatively on the web (Marie-Claire Jerkins)

Microblogging websites have evolved to become a source of varied kind of information

  • People post real time messages about their opinions on a variety of topics (politics, weather, fashion, ..)

  • Companies have started to use this information to get a sense of general sentiment for their product

Platform Overview

Streamer

    Responsible for streaming realtime data to the system and performs initial filtering (duplicate tweets, retweets, non-english tweets).

Classifier

3 steps
  • Feature extraction: a pipeline of filters, used to extract features that greatly describe a phrase
  • Dimensionality reduction: removes low information features that decrease performance
  • Classification: Uses a supervised learning algorithm to train a classifier, that can be used to predict sentiment

Dimensionality Reduction

Process: Remove features that are common across all classes, and therefore contribute little information to the classification process.

This process is also called removing noise

Why: When you use only the higher information features, you can increase performance while also decreasing the size of the model, which results in less memory usage along with faster training and classification.

Dimensionality Reduction

How do we find the most informative features?

  1. Calculate the probability each word appears in different contexts
  2. Score each word with a Chi-square test
  3. Take the top N

Dimensionality Reduction

Calculate Probability distributions

NLTK provides us with these implementations

  • How often have I seen a word?

Dimensionality Reduction

Calculate Probability distributions

  • These can be easily created in O(n)

    
for word, sentiment in phrase.extract_feats():
    fd.inc(word)
    cfd[sentiment].inc(word)

Dimensionality Reduction

Score each word with a chi-square test

Once we have this FreqDist and ConditionalFreqDist, we can score each word on a per-label basis using a chi-square score

  1. The frequency of the word in the label.
  2. The total frequency of the word across all labels.
  3. The total frequency of all words that occurred in the label.
  4. The total frequency for all words in all labels.

Dimensionality Reduction

Take the top N

The bigger the score is, the more informative the feature is

  1. Sort by score in descending order
  2. Take the top N
  3. ..or use a heap ;)
